Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Logitech MK345 compatible with Mac computers?
Yes, the Logitech MK345 is generally compatible with macOS for basic keyboard and mouse functions. However, some specific hotkeys or programmable functions might not work as intended without Logitech Options software, which may or may not fully support this older model.
Does the combo require any special software or drivers?
No, the Logitech MK345 is designed for plug-and-play simplicity. Just insert the included Unifying USB receiver into an available USB port on your computer, and both the keyboard and mouse should work instantly without needing to install any software or drivers.
How long do the batteries actually last?
Logitech claims up to 3 years for the keyboard and 18 months for the mouse. Actual battery life can vary significantly based on usage, environmental conditions, and computing habits. However, users generally report excellent battery longevity, often exceeding a year for both devices.
Is the keyboard noisy when typing?
The MK345 keyboard features standard membrane keys. While not completely silent, they are generally considered to be relatively quiet and produce a soft, comfortable typing sound, making it suitable for shared workspaces or quiet home environments.
Can I use the keyboard and mouse with separate USB receivers?
No, both the keyboard and mouse in the MK345 combo are pre-paired to a single Logitech Unifying USB receiver. You cannot use them with separate receivers; they both operate through the one included USB dongle to save USB ports and simplify setup.
