Frequently Asked Questions

Is the SAMSUNG T7 Shield compatible with all devices?

Yes, the T7 Shield is widely compatible. It works seamlessly with PCs, Macs, Android devices, gaming consoles (like PS4/PS5 and Xbox Series X/S), and even some cameras. It comes with both USB-C to C and USB-C to A cables for broad connectivity.

What does the IP65 rating mean for the T7 Shield?

An IP65 rating means the drive is "dust tight" (no ingress of dust) and protected against "low-pressure jets of water from all directions." While it's resistant to splashes, rain, and dust, it is NOT designed for submersion in water.

How does the T7 Shield handle heat during transfers?

The T7 Shield incorporates Dynamic Thermal Guard technology, which actively monitors and manages the drive's temperature. This ensures that even during long, intensive data transfers, the drive maintains optimal operating temperatures and avoids performance throttling, keeping speeds consistent.

Is the included software necessary?

The SAMSUNG Portable SSD software (available for Windows, Mac, and Android) is optional but recommended for setting up password protection and firmware updates. The drive is plug-and-play without the software, but you'll miss out on the encryption features.

What's the main difference between the T7 and T7 Shield?

The core difference lies in durability. While both offer similar fast performance, the T7 Shield adds a rugged, rubberized exterior with IP65 water/dust resistance and enhanced drop protection (up to 3 meters), making it significantly more robust than the standard T7 model, which is primarily designed for everyday portability.
